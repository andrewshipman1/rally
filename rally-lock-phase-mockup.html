LOCK PHASE V0 · WIREFRAME · 2026-05-03

From sell to lock: the economic transition

Lock phase is the moment soft commitments turn into real money. The organizer stops asking "are you in?" and starts saying "I just paid the deposit, Venmo me $X." Attendees stop browsing and start booking their own travel. This wireframe walks the full journey for both parties — organizer fires lock from sell phase, walks a 7-screen wizard, and then the post-lock trip page transforms into Module A (shared cost commitment) + Module B (personal checklist) for everyone. Strategy doc: rally-lock-phase-strategy-v0.md.

PART 2 · THE WIZARD

7 screens to lock the trip

Tapping lock it in → opens a tall bottom drawer (~85% of viewport). The wizard runs one decision per screen with a thin progress bar at top. Verification first (catches stale sell-state before it gets locked in), then per-item allocation (the binary "I'm booking" / "each attendee books" with inline cost confirmation), then payment handle prompt (only if missing), then final review with editable rows. Allocation choices simultaneously feed Module A (shared costs) and Module B (personal checklists).

2. Wizard screen 1 — verification organizer · wizard 1/7

Pre-flight check. Organizer scans the trip's current state, sets expectations on what's coming. "edit first" exits the wizard and drops them into 9W edit-on-sell mode with a resume lock → affordance. The "have your booking confirmations handy" line preps them for the cost-confirmation steps.

PART 3 · LOCK FIRES

A brief commitment beat

Tapping lock it in 🔒 fires the lock action. Holding members get bumped to out. Cost divisor flips from 6 → 4. actual_cost is finalized on shared items. Lock notifications go out to attendees (email + drip campaign begins). The organizer sees a brief confirmation moment before the trip page transforms into its post-lock composition.

9. Lock-fire moment organizer

Brief celebratory beat — not a heavy interstitial, just a clear "this is real now." Auto-dismisses after ~2s into the new post-lock trip page.

PART 4 · POST-LOCK ORGANIZER VIEW

The trip page transforms

Post-lock, the page composition shifts. PostcardHero compresses (smaller title, no postcard image), a condensed summary card appears with the consequential metadata, the countdown retargets to lock_deadline, and the new two-module surface (A + B) becomes the page's primary content. Existing modules collapse into a trip details ▾ expander. No sticky bottom bar — there's no further action to take on the chrome.

10. Post-lock trip page — organizer view organizer · post-lock

Module A in organizer mode shows aggregate progress ("4 of 4 in" once everyone commits, with per-attendee status indicators). Module B in organizer mode is the per-attendee booking tracker — "who's booked their shit and who hasn't". The crew section evolves to show fractional progress per attendee.

PART 5 · POST-LOCK ATTENDEE VIEW

"i'm in" → checklist → done

Attendees get a notification ("Andrew locked Coachella in — you owe him $4,650"). They open the trip and see two new modules. Module A first: aggregate commitment surface, payment handle, single tap. Module B second: their personal checklist with gated forms (book your flight, book your roommate, etc.). The story is "agree to costs → do your bookings."

11. Attendee opens trip — Module A pre-tap attendee · before commitment

Layout option (c) from gap #3: total prominent + compressed breakdown visible + payment handle below + CTA at bottom. Hierarchy: "you owe $X → here's why → tap to commit → here's how to pay." Payment handle is visible (transparency wins over surprise) but the primary action is committing — Send-Andrew-CTA activates only after the i'm-in tap.

13. Module B item — bottom drawer form open attendee · booking flight

Tapping start → on a Module B item opens a bottom drawer with the gated form. Fields vary per item type — flight needs flight number + arrival time; hotel needs name + roommate confirmation + "who are you rooming with?"; intra-transport needs a confirmation toggle. Self-attestation (the toggle) plus required details (the form) = the price of admission for marking the task done. The Go-phase logistics packet falls out of these fields naturally, no double-entry.

PART 6 · EVERYONE IS IN

The organizer goes to book

All 4 attendees have tapped i'm in. The Module A organizer-mode CTA unlocks: go book the spot →. The organizer now has explicit social cover to charge their card for the shared bookings. Out-of-band, attendees venmo $4,650 each (or wait until the trip — Rally surfaces the path, doesn't enforce it). Module B keeps cooking — attendees gradually fill in their flights, rooming, festival passes. The drip campaign does its job until book by may 12. Then the trip slides into Go phase.

15. Organizer — fully committed, ready to book organizer · 4 of 4 in

Module A flips to "go book the spot →" — the gating event. The organizer has the moat: explicit, documented commitment from every attendee. Module B's per-attendee tracker shows progress in real time. The crew section (in the expander) gets fractional progress per row.
