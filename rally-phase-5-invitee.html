What changes vs. the live trip page

Top line: the live-row swaps from "trip is live" to an inviter row — small avatar of the friend who sent you the link + "jen called you up". Personal, named, social. This is the hook.

Sticker: swaps from countdown to "you're invited 💌". Same shape, different content.

The plan is locked behind a login gate — NOT an RSVP gate. A "the plan" section sits below the going row showing flights / hotel / total per person — but it's blurred behind a Caveat pill that reads "sign in to see the plan ↑". The lock is a login wall, not a commitment. Travel isn't a birthday party — nobody says yes to $800 and 3 days off sight unseen. Once signed in, the full plan is visible, edit affordances off, and RSVP is its own separate decision on the full page.

One open seat in the avatars row. The crew row shows everyone already going + a dashed empty avatar with a tiny "you?" label. Visual reservation.

Sticky bar = big primary CTA + quiet out. Big see the plan → (pulses, triggers login) and a quiet can't make it outline button. The "i'm in" button moves to the full trip page, after they've seen the numbers.

What does NOT change (the chassis is doing the work)

Wordmark, marquee, title, tagline, countdown card, going row, motion language, color theme — all identical to the live trip page. An invitee should immediately understand "this is the same thing my friend has been hyping in the group chat" — because it literally is.

The conversion mechanic, in plain english (updated: login gate, not rsvp gate)

Hook: "[friend] called you up" — a real person, named, with a face. Not "you have been invited to a trip."

Social proof: "5 already in (1 seat with your name)" — frames the invite as a seat held for you, not a calendar event.

Curiosity gap: the plan section is visible but blurred. You can see there are flights, a hotel, a total per person — you just can't read them until you sign in. This is the difference between "tap a button for unknown reasons" and "tap a button to unlock a real thing."

The lock is a login gate, not an RSVP gate. Travel isn't a birthday party. No sane person commits to $800 + 3 days off without seeing the flights, hotel, and total share. So the primary CTA on this screen is see the plan →, not i'm in. Tapping it triggers a passwordless sign-up, unblurs the plan in place, and drops them on the full trip page. They converted to a user. No commitment made yet.

RSVP happens on the full page, after login. The regular trip page sticky bar has a three-state RSVP: i'm in 🙌 · hold my seat 🧗 · can't make it. "Hold my seat" is the critical third option — it's the honest answer for 30% of invitees who need to check with a partner, confirm PTO, or wait on a paycheck. It's not a cop-out, it's an active claim on the reserved seat. The organizer sees 3 in · 2 holding · 1 out instead of 3 yes · 3 blank, which is 10x better intel.

What happens after "see the plan": passwordless sign-up (name + phone or email, one tap), locked section unblurs in place with a count-down-from-blur animation, empty "you?" avatar stays empty (they haven't RSVP'd yet), sticky bar swaps to the 3-state RSVP buttons. Same page, same chassis, new permissions.

What happens after "can't make it": sticker swaps to "maybe next one 👋", the page goes read-only with a small dim overlay, and a soft CTA appears: "start your own rally →". We never punish a no, but we always offer the next step.

Dashboard sync: the dashboard scoreboard chips (Phase 3) will need a fourth state added — "holding 2" — to reflect invitees in the hold-my-seat state. Lexicon §5.10 (RSVP states) needs the three-state spec.

Nudge automations — chase the "hold my seat" and "no response" states until the cutoff

The problem: group travel dies from ambiguity. "hold my seat" and "no response yet" are the two states that kill a trip — not because people said no, but because the organizer can't move to Lock without knowing who's really in.

The feature: every trip has a cutoff date (set by the organizer, e.g., "decide by may 15"). Between invite and cutoff, Rally runs an escalating nudge sequence on anyone still in holding or no response state:

→ T-14 days: soft push notif — "marcus is still holding a seat for you on park city. 4 spots left."

→ T-7 days: slightly more urgent — "last week to decide on park city. the boys are booking flights."

→ T-3 days: the squeeze — "48h left. after friday your seat opens up."

→ Cutoff hit: auto-flip to "can't make it" if no response, with a softer final nudge — "we released your seat — catch the next one?" + a link back to the page in case they want to re-engage.

Why this is actually on-brand and not spam: the nudges come from the friend who invited you, not from Rally. Copy says "marcus is holding a seat" not "Rally wants you to respond." Every nudge carries the friend's name and face. It's a reminder of a social obligation, not a marketing email.

Organizer sees the whole thing as a pipeline. On the trip page in lock-mode, they get a small panel: "3 in · 2 holding · 1 no response · cutoff in 6 days" with per-person nudge history. On the dashboard scoreboard, the "holding" chip might pulse or show "3 holding · 2d left" to pull them back in when the cutoff is close.

This also closes the growth loop. Every nudge is another reason for a non-user to open the app, see the locked page, sign in, and convert. The nudge system is a conversion engine as much as it is a coordination tool.
