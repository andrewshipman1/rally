# Rally — Brand Brief v0

*A working document. One page. Edit anything that's wrong.*
*Andrew Shipman · April 2026*

---

## The position

**Rally is how you get your group to "let's go."**

Group trips are the magnum opus of what good friend groups do. Rally is the daydream you build together, the countdown that makes Tuesday tolerable, and the page your friends can't say no to — not because you sold them, but because you got there first and they want what you're having.

The unit Rally serves is not the individual organizer. It's the **friend group**. The organizer is the rep. The group is the protagonist. Every design and product decision should ask "is this making the group more *itself*?"

## The cultural thesis

Housing is unaffordable. Careers are uncertain. The only investment that pays back reliably is the trip you take with your best friends. Rally is built for the generation that decided experiences are the new equity — and the only thing worth saving up for is the next memory.

Rally isn't a planning tool. It's the artifact of the decision to spend the rent money on the trip anyway.

**And the deeper thing underneath that:** so much of friendship is mediated by phones now that most of what passes for "hanging out" is reacting to each other's stories. Rally exists to push friendships off the algorithm and into the same physical room. We don't want you to live in our app. We want you to live in a hot tub in Vermont with five of your favorite people. The app's job is to make that happen and then get out of the way.

**Rally is a doorway, not a destination.** The DAU goes to zero on the day the trip starts. That's the win, not a failure.

## The core job

**Rally the group to "yes."** Take a vague "we should do something" and turn it into committed RSVPs and a paid Airbnb deposit in 48 hours. Everything else — themes, polls, cost splits — exists to serve that one job.

Rally is the closer your group chat doesn't have. (Not in a sales way. In an *LFG* way. *Let's fucking go.*)

## The three insights the brand is built on

**1. Anticipation is more pleasurable than the experience itself.**
The fantasy phase of a trip — booking the flight, scrolling the Airbnb listing at 3pm — is often the most enjoyable part. Rally is designed to extend and amplify that fantasy phase, not to compress it into a checklist.

**2. The trip page is a re-visitable daydream surface.**
People will open their Rally page dozens of times between booking and going. Every visit needs to reward them — countdown ticking, new RSVPs, new photos, micro-changes. The page is a slot machine for trip dopamine. *Re-openability beats completeness.*

**3. The link is the pitch.**
Rally's distribution mechanism is one URL dropped in a group chat. The page has to do its full job in one tap — every question answered in one scroll, on mobile, on cellular, with zero friction. No app download, no signup, no clicks to find the cost. The page is built for the moment a friend taps a link at 11pm and decides whether to be in. (Screenshots might happen as a side effect — they're not the goal. The goal is the link itself being a complete, self-contained pitch.)

## The voice

**Friend-of-a-friend in the group chat.** Not the brand talking *at* you. Not the brand talking *as* you. The slightly cooler third party who's already been to Lisbon and is texting you what you actually need to know. Knowing but not condescending. Hyped but not cringe. Says "the vibe is immaculate" without irony.

**Voice rules:**
- Never the word "trip planner."
- Never the word "RSVP" if a better word exists.
- Never sound like a calendar app.
- *Let's go* > *commit*. *Vamos* > *confirm*. *We're in* > *RSVP submitted*.
- One step too much > one step too tasteful.

## Personality

**Rally is:** memorable · specific · FOMO-driven · what-you-make-it · edgy · hot-or-cold (no warm middle) · loud · chaotic · hedonistic · nostalgic · urgent · re-openable · self-contained · slightly unhinged

**Rally is not:** tasteful · restrained · corporate · structured · predictable · generic · wedding-website pretty · adult-presenting · spreadsheet-adjacent · "minimal" as a value · Notion-for-trips · wholesome · forgettable

### "What you make it" — unpacked

This is a values claim, not a product claim. Rally is built on the belief that **the right people in the right place beats the perfect itinerary, every time.** Only invite the people who add to the vibe. The trip isn't going to be perfect. Roll with the punches. The flight gets delayed, the restaurant is closed, the weather is wrong — and it doesn't matter, because you're with the right humans and *vacation is fun*. Rally is for the friends who already know that. Rally is not, and will never be, an optimization tool for perfectionists.

The competitors (Wanderlog, TripIt, Troupe) are all chasing perfect itineraries. Rally explicitly isn't. We're chasing the friend group, the showing up, and the stories you'll tell about the day everything went sideways and it was the best day of the trip.

## References

The single most important reference is not a brand. It's **the group chat itself.** That feeling of coming back to your phone after dinner and seeing 60 unread messages, everyone going off, in-jokes flying, you can't catch up — that messy chaotic intimacy is the texture Rally is trying to extend. If Rally feels like a *room* and not an *app*, the room is the group chat.

Brand references that share useful DNA:

- **Partiful** — got the *balance* right between being a real tool and being playful, and figured out how to serve a Gen-Z niche without alienating the 35-year-old who shows up. Take the balance, leave the specific aesthetic.
- **Duolingo** — has a *character* you can recognize in three pixels. Willing to be a little cringe in service of being unmistakable.
- **Glossier** — knows how to talk to its audience without talking down. Voice you'd recognize in a dark alley.
- **Cash App** — irreverent, brutalist, type-too-big, color-too-loud, wins by saying the quiet part loud.

## Design directives (the things to actually do)

**The link is the pitch.** Every Rally page does its full job in one tap on mobile. Cost, house, dates, who's in — answered in one scroll. The 11pm-tap-from-a-group-chat test is the bar.

**Loud, not tasteful.** Bold colors not muted. Type too big not too small. Microcopy too unserious not too clean. Rally should look like a *little too much* in at least one dimension on every screen. Tasteful is the failure mode.

**Optimize every surface for re-openability.** A page someone opens 12 times beats a page that has every field filled in.

**The hero is a postcard, not a header.** Edge-to-edge. Pleasurable to look at. Worth coming back to.

**The lodging carousel is the showroom, not a card.** This is the surface people scroll for dopamine. Treat it like one.

**The countdown is the heartbeat.** Not "time to lock it in." *Days until vino.*

**Mobile-first, but actually.** This product lives on phones, in group chats, opened on cellular. Desktop is a side use case. Every design decision optimizes for the iMessage tap.

**Designed to be closed.** No infinite scroll. No algorithmic feed. No retention hooks begging you back. Rally is a doorway, not a destination. The activity feed exists for re-openability *between* visits — not engagement *during* them. The voice should sound like a friend texting you, not an app trying to retain you.

## The character decision

**Soft character.** Wordmark with attitude (Shrikhand `rally!`, locked) + an emoji sticker kit with uniform treatment (locked v0) + voice rules. No mascot animal. Lower-cost path to Duolingo-level recognizability without the production cost of a true persona.

(Revisit if/when Rally has budget for a proper brand identity engagement. For now: soft character is the right call for Option A scope.)

## The "is the brand on?" test

A surface is on-brand if the answer to all five is yes:

1. If a 26-year-old taps this link cold from a group chat at 11pm on a phone, do they understand the trip in 10 seconds?
2. Does it make at least one person in that chat say "okay yes"?
3. Does it sound like a friend, not a calendar app?
4. Is it a little too much in at least one dimension (color, type, copy, motion)?
5. Would I want to re-open it on a Tuesday at 3pm?

If any answer is no, it's not Rally yet.

## Product implications worth flagging to engineering

These came out of the brand work but they're really product calls:

- **Two countdowns, not one.** The hero countdown is *days until the trip itself* (anticipation). The secondary countdown is *days until commit deadline* (organizer urgency). Currently the PRD only has the second one. The first one is the brand-defining surface.
- **Lodging as gallery, not card.** People scroll Airbnb listings for the dopamine. The lodging section should be the most photo-rich, swipe-rewarding surface on the page. Not a hero card with one image.
- **Re-openability as a product principle.** Replace "fun first" in the PRD with: *"Re-openability over completeness. A page someone opens 12 times beats a page that has every field filled in."*
- **The cover image needs to be edge-to-edge on every breakpoint.** Currently it's a small boxed image floating in theme color on desktop. Kills the postcard effect.
- **Desktop layout is mobile-pretending-to-be-desktop.** Trip pages live in a ~400px column on a 1470px screen. This is where 70% of demos and screenshots happen. Needs a real desktop treatment.

## The wordmark — LOCKED v0

**Rally is set in Shrikhand.** The wordmark reads `rally!` — lowercase word, exclamation point in the accent pink (`#ff2e7e`) on light backgrounds and accent lime (`#c4ff7a`) on dark. The bang is never optional — it's half the logo.

**Usage rules:**
- **Font:** Shrikhand (Google Fonts). No substitutes. No modifications. Never set in DM Sans or Instrument Serif.
- **Weight:** always the same — Shrikhand is single-weight.
- **Case:** always lowercase `rally!`. Never `Rally`, `RALLY`, or `rally` without the bang.
- **Color:** the word is always `--ink` (near-black `#1a1a1a` on light, `#f4f4f5` on dark). The bang is always an accent color (theme-dependent but defaults to `#ff2e7e` pink on light, `#c4ff7a` lime on dark).
- **Minimum size:** 24px (display) / 18px (nav). Below that, drop the bang and use the wordmark `rally` in Shrikhand alone — but this should be avoided where possible.
- **Clear space:** minimum clear space around the wordmark = the x-height of the "r".
- **What not to do:** don't outline it, don't drop-shadow it, don't gradient-fill it, don't animate the letters individually, don't italicize it (Shrikhand is already slanted), don't pair it with another display font in the same composition.

*Locked for v0. Revisit only if a paid brand engagement happens.*

## The sticker kit — emoji-only v0

**v0 ships with no custom illustration kit.** Stickers are **emoji only**, applied with the Rally sticker treatment: rounded pill, bold font, rotation, drop-shadow. This is a deliberate cost call — the sticker treatment is the brand, not the glyph.

**Sticker recipe (the chassis renders this uniformly):**
- Pill: `padding: 6px 14px`, `border-radius: 999px`, `background: var(--sticker-bg)` (theme-dependent, usually accent), `color: var(--sticker-ink)`, `font-weight: 800`, `font-size: 11–13px`, `text-transform: lowercase`, `letter-spacing: 0.04em`, `transform: rotate(-2deg to +2deg)` (randomized or chosen per sticker), `box-shadow: 2px 2px 0 rgba(0,0,0,0.12)`.
- Emoji sits inline with the text: `new rally ✨`, `you're invited 💌`, `just a sketch ✏️`, etc.
- Stickers never animate on load (they're stamps, not motion pieces). Reactions on the buzz feed can animate on add (pop-in), but static stickers don't.

**Deferred:** custom illustrated stickers, a mascot, a sticker marketplace, user-uploaded stickers. If Rally ever gets a proper brand engagement, those are the first thing to revisit.
