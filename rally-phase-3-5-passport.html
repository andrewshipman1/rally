phase 3.5 · the passport · profile surface

the passport.
your trip equity.

The third surface in Rally. Not forward-leaning like the dashboard, not in-the-weeds like the trip page. The passport is identity — the receipt for the rally lifestyle. It's where the brief's "experiences are the new equity" thesis gets visually rendered as a portfolio.

Each stamp = a place + the people you went with. Below: a ride-or-dies leaderboard. Above: three stat blocks (trips, ride or dies, countries). Same chassis, same dual-mode kernel, third pressure test.

Why this surface exists

The brand brief says experiences are the new equity. The trip page is the present, the dashboard is the future, but neither shows the past. The passport does. It's the surface a user opens when they want to feel proud of what they've built — and the surface a friend taps when they want to know "is this person actually fun to travel with?"

The social passport, not the solo one

Airbnb's passport is solo — a list of places you went. Rally's is social — each stamp shows the place and the crew that was there. The avatar dots are mandatory. A stamp without a crew isn't a Rally trip.

What's new on this surface

Stamp grid: 2-column, each tile has its own theme palette pulled from the trip it represents (Tulum is teal, Park City is rust, Lisbon is linen, etc.). Slight rotation per stamp, all wiggling at different phases. Stat blocks: three big Shrikhand numbers with count-up animation on load. Ride or dies leaderboard: pill rows ranking who you've traveled with most. Profile head: the avatar gets a thick border with a contrasting drop-shadow ring (color outline) — same hard-shadow vocabulary as the rest of the kernel.
